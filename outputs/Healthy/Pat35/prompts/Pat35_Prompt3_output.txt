- **T-cell Differentiation and Activation**: The decrease in IL-23 and IFN-Y points to a reduced activation of T-helper cells, which could be a result of a balanced microbiome that fosters T-cell tolerance and prevents excessive inflammatory responses.

- **Bone Health**: The decrease in RANKL and MMP-8 indicates that the inflammatory environment is lessening, which is critical for maintaining the integrity of the periodontal bone structure, potentially influenced by a healthy microbial community that supports tissue repair and homeostasis.

The shifts observed in the biological groups suggest a favorable immune and microbiome profile that supports recovery and maintenance of healthy gums.
